This completes the verification that the Cauchy completion of $\mathbb{Q}$ is a complete ordered field. Uniqueness implies that it is isomorphic to the complete ordered field $\mathbb{R}$ constructed by means of Dedekind cuts in Section 2 of Chapter 1. Decide for yourself which of the two constructions of the real number system you like better – cuts or Cauchy sequences. Cuts make least upper bounds straightforward and algebra awkward, while with Cauchy sequences it is the reverse.
