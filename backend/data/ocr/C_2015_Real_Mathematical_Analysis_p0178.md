The lower integral and upper integral of $f$ over $[a, b]$ are

$$\underline{I} = \sup_{P} L(f, P) \quad \text{and} \quad \bar{I} = \inf_{P} U(f, P).$$

$P$ ranges over all partitions of $[a, b]$ when we take the supremum and infimum. If the lower and upper integrals of $f$ are equal, $\underline{I} = \bar{I}$, then $f$ is Darboux integrable and their common value is its Darboux integral.

20 Theorem Riemann integrability is equivalent to Darboux integrability, and when a function is integrable, its three integrals – lower, upper, and Riemann – are equal.
