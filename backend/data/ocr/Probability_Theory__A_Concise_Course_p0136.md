and hence the second player should guess that the hidden coin is a dime with probability $\frac{5}{13}$, and that it is a quarter with probability $\frac{8}{13}$. Then, according to (4), the first player’s average gain will be

$$V(x^0, y^0) = -10 \cdot \frac{8}{13} + 15 \cdot \frac{5}{13} = -\frac{5}{13},$$

while the second player’s average gain will be

$$-V(x^0, y^0) = \frac{5}{13}.$$

Thus this game is unfavorable to the first player, who loses an average of $\frac{5}{13}$ cents every time he plays, even if he adopts the optimal strategy. However, any departure from the optimal strategy will lead to an even greater loss, if his opponent responds properly.
