$\varphi$ is only piecewise smooth but never mind. If the slope $m$ is 4 then the signed $xy$-area of $\varphi$ is zero. If $m > 4$ it is negative.

$I^2$ has four edges. $\varphi$ sends the bottom edge to itself by the identity map, it sends the top edge to the piecewise linear $V$-shaped path in the plane $z = 1$ from $(0,1,1)$ to $(1/2,1 - m/2,1)$ to $(1,1,1)$. Finally $\varphi$ sends the left and right edges to lines of slope 1 that join $(0,0,0)$ to $(0,1,1)$ and $(1,0,0)$ to $(1,1,1)$. Figure 124 shows the projection of the cell on the $xy$-plane.

**Form Naturality**

It is a common error to confuse a cell, which a smooth mapping, with its image, which is point set – but the error is fairly harmless.

**36 Theorem** Integrating a $k$-form over $k$-cells that differ by a reparameterization produces the same answer up to a factor of $\pm 1$, and this factor of $\pm 1$ is determined by whether the reparameterization preserves or reverses orientation.
