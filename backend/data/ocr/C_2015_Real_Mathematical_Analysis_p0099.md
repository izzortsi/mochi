Connectedness properties give a good way to distinguish nonhomeomorphic sets.

Example The union of two disjoint closed intervals is not homeomorphic to a single interval. One set is disconnected and the other is connected.

Example The closed interval $[a, b]$ is not homeomorphic to the circle $S^1$. For removal of a point $x \in (a, b)$ disconnects $[a, b]$ while the circle remains connected upon removal of any point. More precisely, suppose that $h : [a, b] \to S^1$ is a homeomorphism. Choose a point $x \in (a, b)$, and consider $X = [a, b] \setminus \{x\}$. The restriction of $h$ to $X$ is a homeomorphism from $X$ onto $Y$, where $Y$ is the circle with the point $hx$ removed. But $X$ is disconnected while $Y$ is connected. Hence $h$ cannot exist and the segment is not homeomorphic to the circle.
