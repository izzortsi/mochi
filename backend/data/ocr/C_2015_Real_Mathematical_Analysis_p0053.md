7* Visualizing the Fourth Dimension

A lot of real analysis takes place in $\mathbb{R}^m$ but the full $m$-dimensionality is rarely important. Rather, most analysis facts which are true when $m = 1, 2, 3$ remain true for $m \geq 4$. Still, I suspect you would be happier if you could visualize $\mathbb{R}^4, \mathbb{R}^5$, etc. Here is how to do it.

It is often said that time is the fourth dimension and that $\mathbb{R}^4$ should be thought of as $xyzt$-space where a point has position $(x,y,z)$ in 3-space at time $t$. This is only one possible way to think of a fourth dimension. Instead, you can think of color as a fourth dimension. Imagine our usual 3-space with its $xyz$-coordinates in which points are colorless. Then imagine that you can give color to points (“paint” them) with shades of red indicating positive fourth coordinate and blue indicating negative fourth coordinate. This gives $xyzc$-coordinates.
