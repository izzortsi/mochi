f(1) is $x_{11}$ and $y_1 \neq x_{11}$. Is it $f(2)$? No, because the second digit in the decimal expansion of $f(2)$ is $x_{22}$ and $y_2 \neq x_{22}$. Is it $f(k)$? No, because the $k^{\text{th}}$ digit in the decimal expansion of $f(k)$ is $x_{kk}$ and $y_k \neq x_{kk}$. Nowhere in the list do we find $y$. Nowhere! Therefore the list could not account for every real number, and $\mathbb{R}$ must have been uncountable.
