$$\theta(x, y) = 2 \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec^3 udu - xy.$$

Ora,

$$\int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec^3 udu = \int_{0}^{\arctan y} (\sec u)(1 + \tan^2 u)du =$$

$$= \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec udu + \int_{0}^{\arctan y} (\sec u \tan u) \tan udu.$$

Integrando por partes a segunda parcela, obtemos

$$\int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec^3 udu = \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec udu + \sec(\arctan y) \tan(\arctan y) -$$

$$- \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec^3 udu = \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec udu + xy - \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec^3 udu.$$

Assim,

$$\theta(x, y) = 2 \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec^3 udu - xy = \int_{0}^{\arctan y} \sec udu.$$

Ora,

$$\int \sec udu = \int \frac{\sec u(\sec u + \tan u)}{\sec u + \tan u} du = \ln |\sec u + \tan u| + k,$$

o que nos dĂˇ (note que, no nosso caso, $x + y > 0$)

$$\theta(x, y) = \ln(x + y).$$

Como

$$-\theta(x, y) = \theta(x, -y) = \ln(x - y),$$

obtemos, chamando, carinhosamente, $\theta(x, y)$ de $\theta$,

$$e^\theta = x + y$$

$$e^{-\theta} = x - y$$

(note que, como $x^2 - y^2 = 1$, $x > 0$, nosso $\theta$ atinge todos os reais entre $0$ e $\infty$).

Finalmente, escrevendo $x = \cosh \theta$ e $y = \sinh \theta$, obtemos as expressĂµes

$$\cosh \theta = \frac{e^\theta + e^{-\theta}}{2}, \quad \sinh \theta = \frac{e^\theta - e^{-\theta}}{2}.$$
