the other. In fact, if White always puts the bombs in the lead plane, then Blue will always attack this plane and the resulting probability of the mission succeeding will be 0.8. On the other hand, if White adopts the optimal mixed strategy and puts the bombs in the lead plane only two times out of three, he will increase his probability of accomplishing the mission by $\frac{2}{16}$, since, according to (4),

$$V(x^0, y^0) = \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{8}{10} + \frac{1}{3} \cdot 1 = \frac{13}{15}.$$

By the same token, Blue’s best strategy is to attack the lead plane only one time out of three and the other plane the rest of the time.
